Now let us see how these anti-nutritional substances present in foods, can be eliminated by some simple processes. Now some of these plant chemicals have been shown to be deleterious to health or evidently advantages to human and animal health if consumed after adequate processing.  The chemical and physical characteristics of the anti-nutritional factors, determine the choice of appropriate treatment that is used to eliminate the undesirable compound from foods. Number one is heat treatment. Heat processing is widely accepted as an effective means of inactivating the thermolabile anti-nutritional factors in food material.  This improves the protein quality by inactivating anti- physiological factors particularly trypsin inhibitor and haemagglutinins and by unfolding the protein structure.

Heat treatment processes includes boiling, autoclaving, pressure cooking, extrusion cooking and toasting.

Now boiling, cooking generally inactivates heat-sensitive anti-nutritive factors such as trypsin and chymotrypsin inhibitors and volatile compounds. Cooking for sixty minutes at hundred degrees centigrade was reported to be sufficient to inactivate over ninety percent of the trypsin inhibitor activity in food materials.

The second method is autoclaving, autoclaving under pressure for thirty minutes at one twenty-five degree centigrade and fifteen-pound pressure destroys the thermo-labile inhibitory substances such as cyanogenic glycosides, saponins, terpenoids, and alkaloids.

Now simple pressure cooking. that is pressure cooking under pressure for thirty minutes is known to removes trypsin inhibitors in food.

Then microwave treatment- microwave ovens use frequencies of two point four five and a wavelength is twelve point two centimetres for ten minutes in order to eliminate the trypsin inhibitor and the haemagglutinating activity in food.

Now extrusion cooking is also used to eliminate the entry nutrient factors in foods, the cooking process in this takes place within the excluder where the product produces its own friction and heat due to the pressure that is generated that is ten to twenty bars of pressure. The process can induce both protein denaturation and starch gelatinization as well as, complete inactivation of haemagglutinins in food materials.

Then coming to the next major procedure that is without the use of heat soaking. Soaking could be one of the processes to remove the soluble anti-nutritional factors- seeds soaked in water at twenty two degrees centigrade for a period of eighteen hours were reported to decrease trypsin inhibitor activity in food.

Then germination or sprouting is another procedure used to eliminate, the anti-nutrient factors. Germination has been documented to be an effective treatment to remove some anti-nutritional factors in legumes by mobilizing secondary metabolic compounds. which are thought to function as reserve nutrients. Germination can also lower the phytate content in legume seeds depending upon the type of beam and the germinating conditions.
